Meet Cheryl


I'm Cheryl Wells Collins and

Hi! My name is Cheryl Wells Collins. I am an educator with more than 40 years of experience in education. She began her career as a teacher under the tutelage of her mother-in-law, Marva Collins. She has worked in elementary schools for her entire career eventually served as principal and earning accolades such as ELCA Principal of the Year, and was named a National Distinguished Principal in 2008. In 2017, she assumed the role of Chief Executive Officer for a no for profit organization serving children, youth and their families in under-resourced neighborhoods in Chicago. She offers coaching and consulting services, drawing from her own journey as a leader, wife, and mother of two accomplished adults.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is coaching?
Coaching is a collaborative partnership between a trained professional and an individual seeking support to achieve goals. The coach leads through insightful questions, guiding the coachee to discover their own solutions. Unlike mentoring or counseling, coaching empowers individuals to unlock their inherent answers and potential.
Q: What type of coaching do you provide?
Every area of your life is an opportunity to grow and develop. I can coach you in: career/ work, relationships, work/life balance, family issues, habits, spiritual growth, finances, life transitions, health, and more.
Q: How long is a session?
Typically a coaching session last from 30 to 60 minutes. How many sessions will I need? That depends. Everyone is different. Some people reach goals and milestones quickly and then continue coaching to continue reaching even more goals and milestones. Other people reach goals after four to six sessions and then want to stop coaching sessions. Coaches can stay with the same client for as long as the client feels they are benefiting from the relationship.
